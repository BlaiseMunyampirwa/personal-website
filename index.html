About Me

I am a rising junior at The University of Chicago studying Computer Science and Statistics. I am very interested in software engineering and machine learning. In particular, I am interested in how I can leverage my skills to address problems in finance and the field of health care, especially for underserved communities. In my free time, I love playing and watching soccer with friends, drawing, and watching documentariesa about politics and economics.

I was born and raised in Rwanda where I attended high school before coming to college in 2018.

This summer, I am actively honing my skills in my software engineering internship - at Insightiv Technologies - where I am currently working on developing machine learning models using TensorFlow, Scikit-Learn, and other Python libraries to allow radiologists to perform AI-assisted medical diagnostics in Kigali, Rwanda.

Some Previous Projects

  • LRU-Cache Simulation

    LRU stands for Least Recently Used, which is a common policy used for evictions. In this project, I simulated the behavior of a cache in memory in order to examine the efficiency of data retrieval as well as assess the ratio cache of hits and misses.
    Timeline - April 2020

  • Modeling Cancer Treatment

    In this project, I computationally modelled the effect of dexamethasone (a glucocorticoid drug) on the effectiveness of chemotherapy on patients with Head and Neck Cancer. To inform chemotherapy optimization, I further trained and tested a convolutional neural network to segregate between high and low NR3C1 - gene correlated with activation of cancerous cells - gene expression, which yieled high accuracy.
    Timeline - June 2019 to October 2019

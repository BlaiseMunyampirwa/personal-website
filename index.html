About Me

I am a rising junior at The University of Chicago studying Computer Science and Statistics. I am very interested in software engineering and machine learning. In particular, I am interested in how I can leverage my skills to address problems in finance and the field of health care, especially for underserved communities. In my free time, I love playing and watching soccer with friends, drawing, and watching documentaries about politics and economics.

I was born and raised in Rwanda where I attended high school before coming to college in 2018.

This summer, I am actively honing my skills in my software engineering internship - at Insightiv Technologies - where I am currently working on developing machine learning models using TensorFlow, Scikit-Learn, and other Python libraries to allow radiologists to perform AI-assisted medical diagnostics in Kigali, Rwanda.

Some Previous Projects

  • COVID-19 Tracker Android Application

    In this project I designed an android application for tracking the data related to the spread of COVID-19. The application shows real-time statistics by invoking a REST API call. The application allows a user to get the total number of cases worldwide and by country, number of deaths, recovered and new cases. I am currently improve this application to show data visualization, news articles, etc.
    Timeline - August 2020 - Now

  • LRU-Cache Simulation

    LRU stands for Least Recently Used, which is a common policy used for evictions. In this project, I simulated the behavior of a cache in memory in order to examine the efficiency of data retrieval as well as assess the ratio of cache hits and misses.
